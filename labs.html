E155 – Microcontrollers & FPGA Design

Hands-on embedded systems, ARM, and digital design at Harvey Mudd

This page documents my work in E155: Microcontrollers & FPGA Design. Each lab builds toward real-time, hardware/software systems—starting from GPIO and timers and moving through interrupts, FPGA digital logic, and full embedded system integration.

Across these labs, I worked end‑to‑end on embedded systems, from low‑level ARM assembly and register configuration to FPGA‑based digital design and hardware/software co‑design.

  • • Configuring GPIO, timers, and peripherals at the register level
  • • Designing interrupt‑driven state machines for robust control
  • • Implementing and validating synchronous digital circuits on FPGA
  • • Partitioning functionality between firmware and hardware accelerators
